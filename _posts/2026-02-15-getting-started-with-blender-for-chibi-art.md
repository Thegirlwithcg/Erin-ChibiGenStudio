If you've always wanted to create cute chibi characters in 3D but didn't know where to start, this tutorial is for you.

## What You'll Need

- **Blender 5.0 or later** (free download at [blender.org](https://www.blender.org))
- A mouse with a scroll wheel (highly recommended)
- About 2 hours of free time

## Step 1: Understanding the Chibi Proportions

Chibi characters follow a distinctive set of proportions that differ from realistic human anatomy:

- **Head-to-body ratio**: typically 1:2 or 1:4
- **Large eyes**: taking up about 40% of the face
- **Simplified hands and feet**: mitten-like shapes work great
- **Short limbs**: emphasize cuteness over realism

## Step 2: Setting Up Your Workspace

Before you start modeling, let's configure Blender for character work:

1. Open Blender and delete the default cube
2. Switch to **Front Orthographic** view (Numpad 1)
3. Add a reference image as a background
4. Enable the **Loop Tools** add-on in Preferences

## Step 3: Blocking Out the Shape

Start with simple primitive shapes:

- **Sphere** for the head
- **Cube** (subdivided) for the body
- **Cylinders** for the limbs

> Pro tip: Don't worry about details at this stage. Focus on getting the overall silhouette right first.

## Step 4: Refining the Mesh

Once your blockout looks good from all angles, it's time to refine:

- Add **Subdivision Surface** modifier (level 2)
- Use **proportional editing** for smooth adjustments
- Add **edge loops** where you need more control
